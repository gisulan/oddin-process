3. Working with Mathematicians

How I Lead the Math Team Partnership

My operating principle: I explain the why and what we want to achieve. I give the team clarity on the goal and full freedom to evaluate the best technical options. But always time-framed — to avoid derailing or analysis paralysis — and always evaluated: what was achieved, what's next.

What I Bring to the Table

  • The business problem and the outcome: "eBB margin needs to reach within 1pp of its 6% target" — not "fix the correlation coefficient."
  • The constraints: time, capacity, dependencies. I make the boundaries visible so the team can optimise within them.
  • The evaluation cadence: 2-week sprint reviews. Every cycle ends with a decision — continue, pivot, or escalate — never just a status update.

What I Protect Them From

  • Scope creep from commercial: no "whipsaw" priorities. The roadmap is the shield.
  • Analysis paralysis: if a sprint produces no measurable gain, we pivot to Trading-led mitigation. No shame — it's data.
  • Working in isolation: daily partnership with the Eng Lead to unblock infrastructure constraints immediately. I'm in the room, not waiting for a Jira ticket.
Sprint 1 asks:  Margin decomposition by title/market/bet-type  •  P&L concentration (sharps vs. broad)  •  Top 10 correlation residuals
